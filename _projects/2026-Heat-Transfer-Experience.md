As a mechanical engineer who's career is leaning towards building design, specifically in the HVAC center, the Heat Transfer
course has unsurprisingly been very useful in furthering my intuition of the various aspects of the industry, especially when it comes 
to electrical power being used for heating or cooling. This was especially apparent in one specific problem set from the course,
where we explored the theory behind the efficiency and operation of film heaters and water heaters. In particular, 
two things stood out from that homework. In the first question, we explored the drastic improvement insulation provided to 
the operation of water heaters, and by extension any reservoir of heated or cooled water. In the second, I furthered my 
intuition for the maximum allowable heat flux through a film heater based on a predetermined safe range of operable temperatures.
Both of these questions have direct applications and close analogies to other HVAC machines, specific ones including fin tube
radiators, air handling units, and other fluid machines that I've explored through my experience in commissioning. See my full work
from the problem in the attached PDF.
